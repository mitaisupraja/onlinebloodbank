Donating blood is a noble act that can save lives and help those in need.
When you donate blood, you are giving a precious resource that can be used to
treat patients undergoing surgeries, suffering from medical conditions, or
involved in accidents. The process typically involves a healthcare professional
drawing blood from a vein in your arm using a sterile needle and collecting it
in a bag or container.Afterward, the blood is carefully tested for any infectious
diseases before being used for transfusion.Acceptors are individuals who require a
blood transfusion due to medical reasons.

Blood Groups



A+

A+ blood group has the A antigen on red blood cells and Rh(D) antigen. A+ individuals have anti-B antibodies in their plasma. They can receive blood from A+, A-, O+, and O- donors.

A-

A- blood group has the A antigen on red blood cells but lacks the Rh(D) antigen. A- individuals have anti-B antibodies in their plasma. They can receive blood from A-, and O- donors.

B+

B+ blood group has the B antigen on red blood cells and the Rh(D) antigen. B+ individuals have anti-A antibodies in their plasma. They can receive blood from B+, B-, O+, and O- donors.

B-

B- blood group has the B antigen on red blood cells but lacks the Rh(D) antigen. B- individuals have anti-A antibodies in their plasma. They can receive blood from B- and O- donors.

AB+

AB+ blood group has both the A and B antigens on red blood cells and the Rh(D) antigen. AB+ individuals do not have anti-A or anti-B antibodies in their plasma. They can receive blood from A+, A-, B+, B-, AB+, AB-, O+, and O- donors.

AB-

AB- blood group has both the A and B antigens on red blood cells but lacks the Rh(D) antigen. AB- individuals do not have anti-A or anti-B antibodies in their plasma. They can receive blood from AB-, A-, B-, and O- donors.

O+

O+ blood group does not have the A or B antigens on red blood cells but has the Rh(D) antigen. O+ individuals have both anti-A and anti-B antibodies in their plasma. They can receive blood from O+ and O- donors.

O-

O- blood group does not have the A or B antigens on red blood cells and lacks the Rh(D) antigen. O- individuals have both anti-A and anti-B antibodies in their plasma. They can receive blood only from O- donors.
